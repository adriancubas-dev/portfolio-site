Project

Designing an App for Healthcare Workers

CoviCare — App Design

Overview

Background

CoviCare was a mobile app designed to support healthcare workers during the COVID-19 pandemic. I led the end-to-end app design, creating tools for shift management, mental health resources, and peer support — all tailored to the unique constraints of frontline workers.

Role

Lead App Designer

Skills

App Design, User Research, Prototyping, Design Systems

Project Timeline

4 months

← Back to all work
Problem

Healthcare workers were overwhelmed

During the pandemic, healthcare workers faced extreme stress, long shifts, and limited access to mental health support. Existing tools didn't account for the fragmented schedules and limited free time of frontline workers.

Research

Empathy-driven discovery

Through interviews with nurses and physicians, I learned that any solution needed to be accessible in under 2 minutes, work offline during breaks, and feel supportive rather than clinical.

Design

Quick-access support tools

I designed the app around micro-interactions: 2-minute breathing exercises, one-tap check-ins with peers, and a shift tracker that helped workers maintain boundaries between work and rest.

Core features

  • One-tap mood check-ins with trend visualization
  • 2-minute guided breathing and grounding exercises
  • Anonymous peer support messaging
  • Shift scheduling with rest-period reminders
Outcome

Meaningful support in moments that matter

User testing showed that the app's micro-interaction approach was effective — healthcare workers engaged with the app during short breaks and reported feeling more supported. The peer messaging feature saw particularly high adoption.
