Project

Developing the Onboarding Experience

Quadency — UX Design

Overview

Background

Quadency is a crypto trading platform that aggregates multiple exchanges. New users faced a steep learning curve connecting accounts and understanding automated trading. I designed the onboarding flow to reduce time-to-value and increase activation rates.

Role

UX Designer

Skills

User Research, Information Architecture, Interaction Design, Usability Testing

Project Timeline

3 months

← Back to all work
Problem

Complex setup with high drop-off

New users needed to connect exchange APIs, understand trading bots, and configure portfolios — all before seeing any value. The multi-step setup had a high abandonment rate, with most users dropping off during exchange connection.

Research

Mapping the activation journey

Through funnel analysis and user interviews, I mapped the critical path to the "aha moment" — the point where users first saw their consolidated portfolio across exchanges. I identified that simplifying this path was key to retention.

Design

Progressive disclosure and guided setup

I restructured onboarding around progressive disclosure: show only what's needed at each step, celebrate milestones, and defer advanced configuration until after the user has experienced core value.

Key design principles

  • Step-by-step guided flow with clear progress indicators
  • Inline help and contextual education at decision points
  • Quick-start option to view portfolio before configuring bots
  • Success celebrations at key milestones to build momentum
Outcome

Faster activation and higher retention

The redesigned onboarding reduced time-to-first-value and increased the percentage of users who completed setup and remained active after their first week.
