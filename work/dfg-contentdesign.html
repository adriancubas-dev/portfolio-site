Project

Building a Content Design System

Develop for Good — Content Design

Overview

Background

Develop for Good is a nonprofit that connects student volunteers with nonprofits for tech projects. As the organization scaled, they needed consistent content across multiple platforms and volunteer-facing materials. I built a content design system to ensure voice consistency and empower non-designers to create on-brand content.

Role

Content Designer

Skills

Content Strategy, Design Systems, Voice & Tone, Documentation

Project Timeline

3 months

← Back to all work
Problem

Inconsistent content across touchpoints

With multiple volunteers creating content and no established guidelines, the organization's voice varied wildly across emails, website copy, social media, and application materials. This inconsistency undermined professionalism and confused potential volunteers and nonprofit partners.

Research

Auditing existing content

I conducted a comprehensive content audit across all platforms, identifying patterns, inconsistencies, and the most impactful touchpoints. I also interviewed stakeholders to understand the desired brand personality and organizational values.

Design

A system built for non-designers

I created a content design system that included voice and tone guidelines, content patterns for common use cases, templates for recurring communications, and a taxonomy for organizing information consistently.

System components

  • Voice and tone framework with spectrum examples
  • Content patterns for emails, social, and web
  • Terminology glossary and word list
  • Templates for volunteer communications and partner outreach
Outcome

Scalable consistency

The content design system enabled consistent communication even as the volunteer team grew. New team members could produce on-brand content without extensive onboarding, and the organization's professional presence improved measurably.
