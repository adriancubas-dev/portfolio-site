Project

Integrating Social and Community Features

Mercari — Product Design

Overview

Background

Mercari is Japan's largest marketplace app with over 20 million monthly active users. I led the design for integrating social and community features — including comments, likes, and seller profiles — to increase engagement and build trust between buyers and sellers.

Role

Product Designer

Skills

UX Research, Interaction Design, Prototyping, User Testing

Project Timeline

3 months

← Back to all work
Problem

Users lacked ways to connect

Mercari's marketplace was primarily transactional. Users had limited ways to ask questions about listings, share opinions, or build community around shared interests. This gap led to lower engagement and reduced trust between buyers and sellers.

Research

Understanding user needs

Through user interviews and competitive analysis, I identified key pain points: buyers wanted to ask public questions about items, sellers wanted to build followings, and both groups wanted lightweight social interactions without leaving the marketplace.

Design

Building trust through interaction

I designed a comments system that allowed public Q&A on listings, a likes/favorites feature for community curation, and enhanced seller profiles that showcased reputation and activity.

Key design decisions

  • Public comments visible to all users to build trust through transparency
  • Seller response indicators to show engagement levels
  • Community guidelines integrated into the comment flow
  • Notification system to keep conversations active
Outcome

Increased engagement and trust

The social features led to measurable improvements in listing engagement, buyer confidence, and time spent in the app. Comments became a key trust signal for new buyers evaluating sellers.
